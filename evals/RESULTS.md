# PathwAI - Offline Evaluation Results

Generated by `python -m evals.harness` over 150 deterministic instances (50 seeds x 3 domains), no API keys, no downloads. Every instance is scored against its **A\* optimum**.

## Headline: all planners, offline stub proposer (pooled across domains)

| Planner | Solve rate | Optimality ratio | Avg expansions |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| optimal (A\*) - ground truth | 1.00 | 1.000 | 28.1 |
| uniform-cost (no heuristic) | 1.00 | 1.000 | 79.0 |
| greedy best-first | 1.00 | 1.045 | 15.0 |
| llm (open-loop, no backtrack) | 0.50 | 1.533 | 12.1 |
| **llm + search + verifier** | **1.00** | **1.012** | **25.5** |

Two effects, cleanly separated:

- **Search + verifier buys correctness.** Open-loop `llm` solves only 50% at optimality ratio 1.53; adding search + verification lifts it to 100% at 1.012 - essentially optimal.
- **Guidance buys efficiency.** `llm + search` reaches that optimum while expanding 26 nodes vs. uniform-cost search's 79 - the LLM heuristic focuses the frontier.

## Ablation (the null test): replace the stub with a *random* proposer

| Planner | Solve rate | Optimality ratio | Avg expansions |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| llm (open-loop), random guidance | 0.17 | 1.646 | 11.6 |
| llm + search, random guidance | 1.00 | 1.095 | 65.0 |

Strip the guidance and the two effects dissociate exactly as the theory predicts: the open-loop agent **collapses** (17% solved), while the search+verifier agent still solves everything but its expansions balloon from 26 to 65 - drifting back toward uninformed search. Correctness comes from the verifier; efficiency comes from the guidance.
